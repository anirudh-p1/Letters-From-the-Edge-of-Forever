An essay I wrote exploring Ramanujan's Mock Theta functions, intuitively, for a non-specialist audience. This essay has been entered into various mathematical and science essay competitions.

Summary

In his final letter to G.H. Hardy in 1920, Srinivasa Ramanujan introduced seventeen mysterious functions he called "mock theta functions." For over 80 years, these remained a puzzle to all mathematicians. 
This essay explains:
- The concept of modularity using a pond and fabric analogy to explain how theta functions behave
- Why mock theta functions defy the rules of symmetry
- How Zweger's 2002 discovery of the shadow completed the puzzle
- The role of mock theta functions in counting quantum states of black holes in string theory
